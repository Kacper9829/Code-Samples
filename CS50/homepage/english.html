English Language

English generally follows SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) word order. Modifiers usually come before nouns: a big house, not house big. Questions often invert the subject and auxiliary verb:

English uses tenses extensively(present, past, future) with auxiliary verbs: I have eaten, I will go. Articles: a, an, the – indicate definiteness and specificity. Plurals are usually formed with -s, though there are irregulars (child → children). Pronouns are gendered (he/she) and change depending on case (I/me, he/him).

English borrows heavily from Latin, French, and Germanic roots, making it a bit of a linguistic patchwork. Large vocabulary with synonyms, shades of meaning, and idiomatic expressions. Word order and context are crucial for meaning because English relies less on inflection (word endings) than some languages.
