Polish Language

Polish: very flexible thanks to cases – Jabłko jem or Jem jabłko both work (still means “I’m eating an apple”).

Polish: no articles either (context tells you if it’s “an” or “the”).Verb endings handle tense directly (jem = I eat / am eating; jadłem = I ate). Multiple plural forms: jabłko → jabłka (nom. plural), but different endings depending on case.

Most common, everyday words come from Proto-Slavic., Polish loves prefixes, suffixes, and diminutives:pies (dog) → piesek (doggy, affectionate). książka (book) → książeczka (little book).robić (to do) → zrobić (to complete doing). This makes Polish vocabulary feel dense and flexible, you can create subtle shades of meaning with small changes.
